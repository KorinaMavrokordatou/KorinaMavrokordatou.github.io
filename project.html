Analysis of the black lives matter movement through social media, 2020-2021

Examined through Reddit, Twitter and Instagram data.
Introduction
The Black lives Matter Movement emerged in July 2013, with the use of the hashtag #BlackLivesMatter on social media after George Zimmerman; a neighborhood watch volunteer who killed 17-year old African American Trayvon Martin was acquitted. The movement became nationally recognized for street demonstrations in 2014 and returned to national headlines in June 2020, following the murder of George Floyd by Minneapolis police officer Derek Chauvin. An estimated 15 million to 26 million people participated in the 2020 Black Lives Matter protests in the United States, making it one of the largest movements in the country’s history. Circulation of the hashtag #BlackLivesMatter on social media started many conversations and brought a lot of attention to the number and frequency of police shootings of civilians.
Aims of the project
Through this project, I aim to investigate:

(a) why is the movement necessary;
(b) how have people expressed their opinions on the movement through the Social Media platforms Twitter and Reddit;
(c) how has support for the movement changed between years 2020-2021.
(a) Why is the movement necessary
During 2013-2021, police have killed 9879 people, with 98.3% of killings not resulting in officers being charged with crime. Figure 1 gathers evidence from America's largest 50 cities to prove that crime rates do not determine police violence rates. The regression combines data from 2 different sources; downloaded as a csv file from Mapping Police Violence and Federal Bureau of Investigation, Crime Data Explorer for performing a regression of violent crime rates on police killings rates. The regression yields an R-squared value of 0.035 and a coefficient which is not significant at 5% level; proving that there is no correlation between the 2 variables. Figure 2 then shows Black-White disparity in the same cities; and yields an average of 5.16 Black-White disparity; with values as high as 23.5. Data is again downloaded from Mapping Police Violence and the tooltip added includes 4 variables; City, Black-People killed/100kpopulation, White-People Killed/100kpopulation and Black-White disparity; during 2013-2021. The disparity is also depicted in Figure 3; where it is clear that Black people are killed at much higher rates than white people, given the population structure. The data used was fairly easy to access and download, but a lot of cleaning was required; which was done in Stata. The main challenge faced was the merging of the data which required exactly matching the states; a fairly difficult task when they are not identically formatted.

(b) Social Media data analysis
This was the most challenging part of the project; since as expected, data from Social Media platforms is not as easily accessible. In order to fetch data from Reddit; I had to create a developer’s account and obtain a unique client_id and client_secret, which were used as API keys. I then used PRAW, to fetch the data; and pandas data-frame to store the data in. I then used a combination of PRAW and both TextBlot and Vader libraries to perform a sentiment analysis on the comments of the top 10-scored Reddit posts under the subreddit ‘r/BlackLivesMatter’. I initially signed up for all Facebook, Twitter and Instagram developer accounts but the Facebook API does not allow fetching for specific words and my Twitter developer application was suspended due to it being caught up in one of the spam groups by mistake. Twitter support did not respond to reactivate my key until the 5th of January, when it was too late to update my project. Hence, the Twitter sentiment dataset was downloaded from Kaggle, where the TextBlot library was used to provide sentiment analysis. However, the dataset required a lot of cleaning, which was performed in Stata.

Reddit’s score represents the number of upvotes minus the number of downvotes, hence as seen in Figure4, the movement gained a lot of positive attention during 2021, with the top-scored post at 15864. Reading through the top-20 scored posts it’s obvious that the tone varies, from serious to lighter tones and positives to negatives. The complementary Figure5 supports this statement as it can be seen that there is a lot of variation in the comment section of the posts examined; with the majority of the posts having a neutral tone. I used both libraries since I wanted to test how each one works.
